About Restaurant

Since 1995, Shahi's Diner has been bringing a unique experience to the diner franchise restaurant category. As a business with over 140 locations in 14 states and counting, our brand values, culture and heritage are ready to position you for success. All trails lead to a franchise opportunity that satisfies your hunger for success.

Born in the small mountain town of Mount Shasta, CA, founders Bruce Dean and Bob Manley created their first diner with a black bear theme. They wanted it to be a place where family and friends could be served home-cooked meals with a focus on providing guests with exceptional service. Their vision also included a community-centered atmosphere, where small-town values like honesty, integrity and hard work were honored. Over 25 years later, Shahi's Diner is thriving and still serving the same genuine experience.We love to give back to the communities that support our local Black Bear Diners. Our Dine & Donate is a community/grassroots fundraising program that allows us to partner up with local organizations, schools or churches looking for an idea to raise funds for their cause or purpose.


Our History

Started in 2018, Shahi's Diner supports the Nicaraguan families that grow the single-origin coffee we serve in our restaurants. The program provides support for infrastructure, medical and educational-focused activities. Beginning as an internal program, it quickly gained momentum. Today, our guests can support this important endeavor by purchasing bags of the 100% Nicaraguan coffee at our Diners, with all profits going to fund mountain child initiatives.

Our first project is well pump improvement and piping construction. This new system will bring easily accessible running water to the homes in Mancotal, a community where many of our beans are grown. Shahi's dinner is also funding a medical care van to visit this relatively remote village where routine medical care is not available and providing school supplies to the children.


Three Shahi's

3 Shahi's is a fundraiser in partnership with Gold Country Wildlife Rescue focusing on providing rehabilitative care to three bears rescued during the 2021 Dixie Fire. Since we announced our support of these three cubs, the Black Bear Community has raised $17,300 which will go toward enclosures and facility improvements. If you’d like to learn more about this project visit goldcountrywildliferescue.org
